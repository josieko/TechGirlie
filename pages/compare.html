Coming back to Samantha - Let's compare

This time it’s Samantha REST (Samantha R) vs Samantha GraphQL (Samantha G).

You would ask each of them in a different way to get you a publisher.

Samantha R might have different endpoints for getting you a list of all the publishers she knows, another endpoint for a specific publisher and an endpoint for actually submitting a manuscript to a publisher.

For example, if you want to take your chance and get a list of all the publishers she knows, you would place your request to the “all publishers” endpoint.

Samantha would then process your request and return a response in JSON format containing the list of publishers, or the information about the specific publisher you requested, or the status of your manuscript submission.

With Samantha G, things are a bit different. This Samantha has a single endpoint that you can use to ask her for publishers, and to find a publisher for your manuscript, you would send a GraphQL query to the endpoint, specifying the data you need.

For example, you might send a query that asks for the names and contact information of all publishers who are interested in publishing mystery novels (genre of your book).

Samantha would then parse and validate your query/request, and then fetch the requested data from her database. She would then return a response in JSON format, containing the information you requested (names and contact information) about all the publishers doing mystery novels.
